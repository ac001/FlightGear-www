December 20, 2002 - Wright Flyer model complete
Just in time for the start of the Centenial year of powered flight, FlightGear ships with a rather nice model of the 1903 Wright Flyer. The flight dynamics model was developed by Michael Selig at the University of Illinois, Urbana Champaign. The visual model started life as an MSFS aircraft, but was substantially reworked, enhanced, and animated by Jim Wilson of the FlightGear project.

December 20, 2002:
Two papers were recently presented at the European Space Agency ASTRA 2002 conference. Both projects used FlightGear as the frame work for plugging in their research. One paper involves navigation of autonomous vehicles on foreign planets using terrain recognition; the other discusses the development of a fuzzy autopilot designed to control an autonomous flying vehicle operating in the Mars atmosphere. Both can be found in the Papers section of the Documenation page
