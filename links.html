FGFS Related Links


Flight Gear Web Pages


These pages are maintained by FlightGear contributors and may directly or indirectly contain FlightGear info.
  • The official Flight Gear page.
  • JSBSim Flight Dynamics Modeling Site - JSBSim is an object-oriented, multi-platform, flight dynamics model written in C++. It is one of the several ways to drive aircraft flight "math/physics" for FlightGear, but JSBSim also can be run in a standalone mode and is also being used for other projects. The JSBSim web site also strives to serve as a resource to flight simulation engineers, hosting and providing links to relevant technical papers and references to other sites.
  • Robin Peel's Airport and Navaid data homepage.
  • Dave Luff's TaxiDraw. TaxiDraw is a taxiway editor for FlightGear and X-Plane, written by David Luff.
  • FlightGear World Custom Scenery Project
  • Jon Stockill's FlightGear Scenery Object Database.
  • Flight Gear Aviation Training Device project (FAA Certification sought)
  • Atlas - Cartography for virtual worlds. A FlightGear compatible moving map display and atlas.
  • Erik Hofman's prebuilt sgi binaries.
  • Aircraft Dynamics Models for Use with FlightGear - Maintained by Dr. Michael Selig, UIUC.
  • OpenGC - Open source glass cockpit display project.
  • FlightGear Developer Locations - View a map of the world with the locations of the flightgear developers who have volunteered an approximate lon/lat.
  • SpaceGear.org - Open Standards for Space Flight Simulation.
  • FGFS Online Flight Zone
  • A Japanese language translation of the FlightGear website.
  • Signs: Displays city/airport/object names as floating text within the FlightGear visuals. Requires no changes to the FG code, but requires write access to the FlightGear data tree.
  • The FlightGear AI Aircraft Download Page hosted by Durk Talsma.
  • Fred's "current" builds of FlightGear from cvs as well as the FlightGear Scenery Designer, Taxidraw, Atlas, and a few other miscellaneous things.
  • FlightGear Flight Planner - A Java/SWT based Gui flight planner which uses the airport and navaid databases of Flightgear, allowing the user to search for airports and navigation aids and plan flights between them.
  • FGMS - a standalone network server for the FlightGear flight simulator, licenced under the GPL. With fgms you can fly with other pilots scattered around the world. You can assemble experiences in formation flight, build virtual airlines, track yourself on google maps, etc.
  • A Russian language site about FlightGear.

Other Flight Sim Related sites on the Net


  • The Flightsim Aviation Zone - Source for aviation and flight simulator related news and forums.
  • Gene Buckle is working on building his own lifesize flight sim out of an old airplane fuselage. He hopes to drive the display and instruments with Flight Gear.
  • LaRCsim - A set of ANSI C routines that implement a full set of equations of motion for a rigid-body aircraft in atmospheric and low-earth orbital flight, suitable for pilot-in-the-loop simulations on a workstation-class computer.
    You should click on the "Free Utilities", fill in the form to declare your USA citizenship, and then you can download LaRCsim.
  • Nasa Ames Research Center - The Aeronautical Test and Simulation Division at Ames Research Center operates two world class piloted flight simulation laboratories, the Vertical Motion Simulator (VMS) and the Crew Vehicle Systems Research Facility (CVSRF).
  • Silent Wings - Soaring Simulator.
  • Eagles - A Heli sim with impressive looking terrain and helicopters. (DOS)
  • ACM - Air Combat Maneuvers. A neat combat sim. (Unix/X11)
  • Vertigo - Another promising sim that models several different aircraft and has some decent graphics. (DOS)
  • Build your own full motion cockpit
  • FGFLY - a public domain flight simulator. MS-DOS only.
