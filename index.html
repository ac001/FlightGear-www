Latest News

  • June 5, 2003: Version 0.9.2 is available for download (requires SimGear-0.3.3.) Download here.
  • January 29, 2003: FlightGear wins the Linux Game Tome's award for the Best Free Simulation. Congratulations everyone! The runner up in the voting was FooBillard. This is a billard game for linux. I don't know if it ended up in the most appropriate category, but it is certainly worth taking a look at. It's a great way to kill a few minutes (or more) while you are waiting for FlightGear to compile. The authors have done a very nice job with this game, great physics, beautiful rendering, and very entertaining. :-)
  • December 20, 2002: Just in time for the start of the Centenial year of powered flight, FlightGear ships with a rather nice model of the 1903 Wright Flyer. The flight dynamics model was developed by Michael Selig at the University of Illinois, Urbana Champaign. The visual model started life as an MSFS aircraft, but was substantially reworked, enhanced, and animated by Jim Wilson of the FlightGear project.
  • December 20, 2002: Two papers were recently presented at the European Space Agency ASTRA 2002 conference. Both projects used FlightGear as the frame work for plugging in their research. One paper involves navigation of autonomous vehicles on foreign planets using terrain recognition; the other discusses the development of a fuzzy autopilot designed to control an autonomous flying vehicle operating in the Mars atmosphere. Both can be found in the Papers section of the Documenation page

Project Summary

The FlightGear flight simulator project is an open-source, multi-platform, cooperative flight simulator development project. Source code for the entire project is available and licensed under the GNU General Public License.

The goal of the FlightGear project is to create a sophisticated flight simulator framework for use in research or academic environments, for the development and pursuit of other interesting flight simulation ideas, and as an end-user application. We are developing a sophisticated, open simulation framework that can be expanded and improved upon by anyone interested in contributing.

There are many exciting possibilities for an open, free flight sim. We hope that this project will be interesting and useful to many people in many areas.
